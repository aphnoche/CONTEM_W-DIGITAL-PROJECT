TITLE: INTERNATIONAL TURNABOUT
Estimated Playtime: 10-15 minutes
Player Character: Deputy Prosecutor Niang (ICC Prosecution)

[SCENE START]
DATE: February 23, 2026
LOCATION: The Hague, Netherlands - ICC Pre-Trial Chamber I
BGM: [Tense, formal courtroom music plays]

(Screen fades in on the high ceilings of the International Criminal Court. The atmosphere is heavy.)

JUDGE MOTOC:
(Gravely, banging the gavel once)
This Pre-Trial Chamber of the International Criminal Court is now in session. We are here for the Confirmation of Charges hearing in the case of the Prosecutor v. Rodrigo Roa Duterte.

JUDGE MOTOC:
I must remind the gallery: The purpose of this four-day hearing is to determine whether there are "substantial grounds to believe" that Mr. Duterte committed the crimes charged. We are not determining guilt or innocence today.

PROSECUTOR NIANG (PLAYER):
(Nods confidently)
The Prosecution is ready, Your Honor.

DEFENSE KAUFMAN:
(Adjusts glasses, looking stern)
The Defense is present, Your Honor. Though under protest.

JUDGE MOTOC:
Noted. For the record, the suspect is currently detained at the ICC Detention Centre in Scheveningen following his arrest in Manila on March 11, 2025. He has waived his right to appear in person but is monitoring the proceedings via video link.

(Screen cuts to a monitor displaying FORMER PRESIDENT DUTERTE, looking impassive, wearing a suit.)

JUDGE MOTOC:
Prosecutor Niang, you may read the charges.

PROSECUTOR NIANG:
(Slams hands on the desk)
Your Honor! The Prosecution charges Rodrigo Duterte with Crimes Against Humanity—specifically, murder and attempted murder. These acts were committed between November 1, 2011, and March 16, 2019, during his tenures as Mayor of Davao City and President of the Philippines, under the guise of a so-called "War on Drugs."

DEFENSE KAUFMAN:
(Points finger accusingly)
OBJECTION!

(Screen shakes)

DEFENSE KAUFMAN:
This court is overstepping its bounds! The Republic of the Philippines withdrew from the Rome Statute. That withdrawal became final on March 17, 2019. You have zero jurisdiction here! My client's transfer to The Hague last year was nothing short of an extraordinary rendition—a kidnapping that violated Philippine sovereignty!

JUDGE MOTOC:
(Frowns)
A bold claim, Mr. Kaufman. Prosecutor Niang, the Defense challenges our very right to hold this hearing. How do you respond to the question of jurisdiction?

[CHOICE 1: Defend the Court's Jurisdiction]
(Select the correct legal argument to counter the Defense)

OPTION A: "The ICC has universal jurisdiction over any country in the world, regardless of treaties."
OPTION B: "Article 127 of the Rome Statute states that withdrawal does not discharge a state from obligations for crimes committed before the withdrawal took effect."
OPTION C: "The suspect was arrested on an international warrant, which legally overrides national sovereignty."

(If Player chooses A or C:)
JUDGE MOTOC: (Shakes head) That is legally inaccurate, Prosecutor. The ICC is bound by the Rome Statute, not universal jurisdiction.
(Penalty to player health/credibility)

(If Player chooses B:)
PROSECUTOR NIANG:
(Smirks, tapping a document)
TAKE THAT!

(Screen flashes white)

PROSECUTOR NIANG:
The Defense seems to be ignoring Article 127 of the Rome Statute. Yes, the Philippines withdrew in 2019. However, the law clearly states that withdrawal does not absolve a state of criminal liability for acts committed while they were still a member.

PROSECUTOR NIANG:
Our charges span strictly from 2011 to March 16, 2019—the exact period the Philippines was an active State Party!

JUDGE MOTOC:
(Nods)
The Prosecution is correct. The Appeals Chamber has repeatedly upheld this Pre-Trial Chamber's jurisdiction on this very basis. The objection is overruled. Prosecutor, please call your first witness.

[SCENE 2: The Cross-Examination]
BGM: [Suspenseful investigation track]

PROSECUTOR NIANG:
The Prosecution calls an independent investigator from Human Rights Watch to the stand.

(A WITNESS takes the stand, looking resolute but tired.)

WITNESS (HRW INVESTIGATOR):
I have spent years documenting the "War on Drugs." Official government statistics state that over 6,000 people died in anti-drug operations. However, independent domestic monitors, civil society groups, and our own investigations estimate the toll to be between 12,000 and 30,000.

PROSECUTOR NIANG:
Can you describe the nature of these killings?

WITNESS:
They were not random. There was a clear, systematic modus operandi. Police or unidentified assailants would target alleged drug users—mostly from low-income, marginalized communities. We documented 49 specific incidents involving 78 victims for this preliminary hearing.

DEFENSE KAUFMAN:
OBJECTION!

(Defense desk slam)

DEFENSE KAUFMAN:
"Systematic?" "Targeted?" These are inflammatory words! The police were conducting legitimate law enforcement operations! When officers are fired upon by dangerous drug syndicates, they have a right to self-defense!

JUDGE MOTOC:
Prosecutor, it is true that a state has the right to enforce its domestic laws. How do you prove this was a Crime Against Humanity and not simply violent resistance to law enforcement?

[CHOICE 2: Prove the "Crime Against Humanity" Threshold]
(Select the piece of evidence that disproves the "Self-Defense" narrative)

OPTION A: Present the autopsy reports showing victims were shot in the back and possessed no weapons.
OPTION B: Argue that drugs are not actually a problem in the Philippines.
OPTION C: Point out that some police officers were convicted in Philippine domestic courts.

(If Player chooses B:)
PROSECUTOR NIANG: The drug problem is a myth!
DEFENSE KAUFMAN: Nonsense! The drug crisis was a severe national security threat documented by multiple administrations. You are ignoring reality! (Penalty)

(If Player chooses C:)
PROSECUTOR NIANG: Domestic courts convicted nine police officers!
DEFENSE KAUFMAN: Exactly! The domestic justice system works! Therefore, the ICC shouldn't be interfering! (Penalty - this actually helps the defense!)

(If Player chooses A:)
PROSECUTOR NIANG:
(Points dramatically)
HOLD IT!

PROSECUTOR NIANG:
If these were merely cases of "self-defense," why does the physical evidence tell a completely different story?

(A document appears on screen: 'EVIDENCE: Forensics Summary')

PROSECUTOR NIANG:
We have forensic evidence and eyewitness testimony showing that victims were frequently unarmed. Autopsies reveal many were shot execution-style, execution wounds to the head and back. Furthermore, we have evidence of "planted" weapons and drugs at the crime scenes.

PROSECUTOR NIANG:
Under international law, a Crime Against Humanity requires a "widespread and systematic attack directed against any civilian population, with knowledge of the attack." This was a state policy, not an accident.

WITNESS:
That is correct. Furthermore, we have public broadcasts of the suspect explicitly inciting this violence.

[SCENE 3: The Suspect's Voice]
BGM: [Tense, slow tempo track]

DEFENSE KAUFMAN:
(Scoffs)
Public broadcasts? You mean political rhetoric! My client is famous for his colorful language. You cannot prosecute a man for hyperbole!

JUDGE MOTOC:
I would like to hear from the suspect regarding this "rhetoric." Mr. Duterte, you are joining us via video link from the Scheveningen Detention Centre. Do you have a statement?

(Screen cuts back to DUTERTE via video link)

DUTERTE (VIA VIDEO):
(Speaks slowly, defiantly)
I do not recognize this court. But I will say this to the world: I did what I had to do to protect my country from being destroyed by drugs. I told the police, if they pull a gun on you, kill them. If I am responsible for the deaths of criminals, I take full responsibility. But I am not guilty of crimes against humanity. I am guilty of loving my country.

DEFENSE KAUFMAN:
(Nods)
You see? He was protecting his citizens. The police were given rules of engagement: use lethal force only when threatened.

PROSECUTOR NIANG:
(Grits teeth)
Is that so?

[CHOICE 3: The Final Rebuttal]
(Counter the defense's claim that Duterte only authorized legal rules of engagement)

OPTION A: Demand that Duterte be forced to fly to the court immediately.
OPTION B: Present video evidence of Duterte's 2016 speeches ordering the slaughter of suspects without trial.
OPTION C: Request a 60-day extension to find more witnesses.

(If Player chooses A or C:) (Judge Motoc shakes her head, warning the Prosecutor to stay on topic. Penalty.)

(If Player chooses B:)
PROSECUTOR NIANG:
(Slams desk)
OBJECTION!

(Screen flashes white)

PROSECUTOR NIANG:
Let the record reflect the truth, not the sanitized version the Defense is trying to sell today!

(A video transcript appears on screen: 'EVIDENCE: Public Speeches 2016')

PROSECUTOR NIANG:
In 2016, the suspect did not tell the police to follow the rules of engagement. He publicly stated: "If I become the president, I will order the military and the police to hunt down the drug lords... and kill them." He offered bounties. He promised to pardon police officers who faced domestic charges for these killings.

PROSECUTOR NIANG:
This was not hyperbole; it was a green light for impunity! The rhetoric became state policy. The state policy resulted in thousands of extrajudicial killings. The burden of proof for a confirmation of charges has been overwhelmingly met!

[SCENE 4: The Verdict Awaits]
BGM: [Calm, resolution track]

DEFENSE KAUFMAN:
(Sweating slightly, but stands firm)
Your Honor, the Prosecution has cherry-picked statements and relied on biased NGO reports. The Philippines is a sovereign nation that handled an internal crisis. Confirming these charges sets a dangerous precedent for any world leader dealing with domestic security!

PROSECUTOR NIANG:
(Adjusts collar)
No, Your Honor. Confirming these charges sets a precedent that no one—no matter their political power—is above international law. The families of the victims sitting in the gallery today have waited nearly a decade for this moment. Truth is the antidote to impunity.

JUDGE MOTOC:
(Raises hand to silence the room)
The Chamber has heard sufficient arguments from both the Prosecution and the Defense.

(Judge Motoc looks over her documents)

JUDGE MOTOC:
We have examined the arguments regarding jurisdiction, the nature of the alleged crimes, the claims of self-defense, and the suspect's public statements.

JUDGE MOTOC:
Under the Rome Statute, this Chamber now has 60 calendar days to deliberate. We will issue a written decision on whether to decline the charges, request amendments, or confirm the charges and commit Mr. Duterte to a full trial before a Trial Chamber.

JUDGE MOTOC:
Until then, the suspect’s appeals for interim release have been rejected due to flight risks. He shall remain in custody at the ICC Detention Centre in The Hague.

JUDGE MOTOC:
This hearing is adjourned.

(Bangs Gavel)

(Screen fades to black)

The confirmation of charges hearing concluded in late February 2026. As the ICC deliberates on whether to proceed to a full trial, the debate over sovereignty, international law, and human rights continues to echo from Manila to The Hague.
