The Plot: "The Whistleblower's Drive"
Your Role: You are an investigative journalist.
The Premise: A mysterious, highly encrypted USB drive has been leaked to you by an anonymous whistleblower inside a notorious (fictionalized) detention center called "San Otero." The drive contains a directory of 20 encrypted audio logs and text files detailing a massive cover-up of human rights abuses.
The Threat (The "Bombs"): ICE Cyber-Security units have embedded tracking algorithms (the "bombs") throughout the drive's directory. If you click on a file containing a tracker, the ICE system detects your intrusion, wipes the drive remotely, and the story is lost forever.
The Goal: You must use logic to carefully decrypt the safe files (clearing the board). Your ultimate goal is to safely extract all 20 testimonies to publish a career-defining exposé that will trigger a federal investigation and shut down San Otero.

The 20 Story-Driven Dialogues
Instead of random facts, these dialogues now follow a chronological narrative arc. As the player decrypts more tiles, they piece together a specific timeline of events: terrible conditions leading to a sickness, a peaceful protest, and a violent cover-up.

Tying the Game Mechanics to the Plot
Flags: You place a "Proxy Server" or an "Encryption Key" to safely mark and lock down an ICE tracker without triggering it.

The Numbers: If a tile says "3," it means "There are 3 ICE tracking algorithms in the adjacent files."

Game Over Screen: "CONNECTION SEVERED. ICE Cyber-Security has detected your intrusion. The drive has been remotely wiped. The truth remains buried."

Victory Screen: "DOWNLOAD COMPLETE. You have successfully decrypted the San Otero files. Your exposé is published, prompting an immediate Congressional hearing and the closure of the facility."
